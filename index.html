Research

Can State Capitalism Promote Innovation? Evidence from China (Job Market Paper)

I find China’s state capitalism policy creates large productivity and welfare loss. To show this, I develop an endogenous growth model of state-owned and private firms. In the model, political connections grant state-owned firms better factor market access. Such privilege leads to higher return to R&D investment for state-owned firms. Furthermore, the competitive pressure from privileged state-owned firms lowers the return to innovation for private firms, thus discouraging them from investing in R&D. I structurally estimate the model using Chinese industrial firm data. Results show that state-owned firms are inefficient in producing innovation. Nevertheless, they invest more in R&D than private firms due to their higher expected return. Removing state-owned firms’ privileged market access increases annual productivity growth by 1.2 percentage points and total welfare by 23%.
